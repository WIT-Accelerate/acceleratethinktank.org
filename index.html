CHALLENGE

“In 2017 Boston reached a tipping point, where we have more people over the age of 60 than children under the age of 18 – and we will never go back.”

– Emily Shea, Commissioner on Affairs for the Elderly/City of Boston

This is true across the world. Populations in Asia and Europe are aging at a much more rapid rate than in the US and people have never lived this long according to the World Health Organization. This, however, does not necessarily mean that they are living healthier or societally integrated. Aging is still stigmatized and our views towards aging can be challenged towards respect and social inclusion and we can redefine how we take advantage of these additional years of living.

THINKTANK - PREPARE TO BE INSPIRED

It is critical to think broadly across sectors and communities as a variety of aspects of public life are impacted such as healthcare, social services, work and retirement, transportation, mobility, housing, community building, social integration and others to improve the overall quality of life.

For one day, industry thought leaders, students, and faculty from diverse disciplines will engage together with design thinkers, community partners, and city representatives to explore and co-create opportunity spaces and ideas around the Future of Aging.

We are thrilled to convene this event as a partnership between Accelerate - Wentworth Innovation + Entrepreneurship Center, Innovate@BU, Cambridge Innovation Center (Venture Café/Captains of Innovation), Business Innovation Factory, General Electric and the Massachusetts Executive Office of Elder Affairs.
